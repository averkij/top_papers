1. This article explores the expansion of the capabilities of large language models (LLMs) to enable them to generate 3D meshes within a unified model.

2. The main advantages include leveraging the spatial knowledge already present in LLMs and achieving conversational 3D generation and mesh understanding.

3. The key challenge is to effectively convert 3D mesh data into discrete symbols that LLMs can process.

4. To address this, we introduce LLaMA-Mesh, a new method that represents the vertex coordinates and face definitions of 3D meshes as pure text.

5. Through this method, LLMs can generate 3D meshes, produce alternating text and 3D mesh outputs, and understand and interpret 3D meshes.

6. Our work demonstrates for the first time that LLMs can acquire complex spatial knowledge through fine-tuning for 3D mesh generation, effectively unifying 3D and text modalities.

7. The mesh generation quality of LLaMA-Mesh is comparable to models trained from scratch, while maintaining strong text generation performance.
