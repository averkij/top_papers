Towards Understanding Camera Motions in Any Video

1. 我们介绍了 CameraBench，一个大规模的数据集和基准，旨在评估和改进摄像机运动理解。

2. CameraBench 包含约 3,000 个多样化的网络视频，由专家通过严格的多阶段质量控制过程进行标注。

3. 我们与摄影师合作，设计了摄像机运动基元的分类法。

4. 例如，某些运动如“跟随”需要理解场景内容，如移动的主体。

5. 我们进行了大规模的人类研究，以量化人类标注性能，发现领域专业知识和基于教程的培训可显著提高准确性。

6. 使用 CameraBench，我们评估了结构从运动（SfM）和视频语言模型（VLMs），发现 SfM 模型难以捕捉依赖场景内容的语义基元，而 VLMs 难以捕捉需要精确轨迹估计的几何基元。

7. 我们随后在 CameraBench 上微调了一个生成 VLM，以实现两者的优势结合，并展示其应用，包括运动增强的字幕、视频问答和视频文本检索。

8. 我们希望我们的分类法、基准和教程能推动未来努力，实现理解任何视频中摄像机运动的最终目标。

1. Wǒmen jièshào le CameraBench, yīgè dàguīmó de shùjùjí hé jīzhǔn, zhǐyǐn pínggǔ hé gǎijìn shèxiàngjī yùndòng lǐjiě

8. Wǒmen xīwàng wǒmen de fēnlèi fǎ, jīzhǔn hé jiàoxué néng tuīdòng wèilái nǔlì, shíxiàn lǐjiě rènhé shìpǐn zhōng shèxiàngjī yùndòng de zhòngdiǎn mùbiāo

1. We introduce CameraBench, a large-scale dataset and benchmark aimed at evaluating and improving the understanding of camera motion.

2. CameraBench contains approximately 3,000 diverse web videos, annotated by experts through a rigorous multi-stage quality control process.

3. We collaborated with photographers to design a classification scheme for camera motion primitives.

4. For instance, certain motions like "following" require an understanding of scene content, such as moving subjects.

5. We conducted large-scale human studies to quantify human annotation performance and found that domain expertise and tutorial-based training can significantly improve accuracy.

6. Using CameraBench, we evaluated Structure-from-Motion (SfM) and Video Language Models (VLMs), finding that SfM models struggle to capture semantic primitives dependent on scene content, while VLMs struggle with geometric primitives that require precise trajectory estimation.

7. Subsequently, we fine-tuned a generative VLM on CameraBench to combine the strengths of both, demonstrating its applications, including motion-enhanced captioning, video question answering, and video-text retrieval.

8. We hope that our classification scheme, benchmark, and tutorials will drive future efforts towards the ultimate goal of understanding camera motion in any video.
