On Domain-Specific Post-Training for Multimodal Large Language Models

1. 近年来，通用多模态大语言模型（MLLMs）取得了快速发展。

2. 然而，将这些通用模型应用到特定领域，如科学和工业，仍然较少探索。

3. 本文系统地研究了通过后训练进行MLLMs的领域适应，重点是数据合成、训练流水线和任务评估。

4. 我们开发了一个视觉指令合成器，能够从特定领域的图片-标题对中生成多样化的视觉指令任务。

5. 我们的合成任务在增强MLLMs的领域特定性能方面优于手动规则、GPT-4和GPT-4V生成的任务。

6. 我们还使用单阶段训练流水线来增强特定领域的任务多样性。

7. 我们在生物医学和食品领域进行了实验，并评估了不同来源和规模的MLLMs在各种特定领域任务上的性能。

8. 为支持进一步研究，我们将开源我们的实现。

1. Jìn nián lái, tōngyòng duō mó tài dà yǔyán móxíng (MLLMs) qǔdé le kuàisù fāzhǎn

2. Rán'ér, jiāng zhèxiē tōngyòng móxíng yìngyòng dào tèdìng lǐngyù, rú kēxué hé gōngyè, réngrán jiàoshǎo tànsuǒ

3. Běnwén xìtǒng de yánjiū le tōngguò hòu xùnliàn jìnxíng MLLMs de lǐngyù shìyìng, zhòngdiǎn shì shùjù héchéng, xùnliàn liúshuǐxiàn hé rènwù pínggǔ

4. Wǒmen kāifā le yīgè shìjiào zhǐlìng héchéngqì, nénggòu cóng tèdìng lǐngyù de túpiàn-biāotí duì zhōng shēngchéng duōyànghuà de shìjiào zhǐlìng rènwù

5. Wǒmen de héchéng rènwù zài zēngqiáng MLLMs de lǐngyù tèdìng xìngnéng fāngmiàn yōu shǒudòng guīzé, GPT-4 hé GPT-4V shēngchéng de rènwù

6. Wǒmen hái shǐyòng dān jiēduàn xùnliàn liúshuǐxiàn lái zēngqiáng tèdìng lǐngyù de rènwù duōyànghuàxìng

7. Wǒmen zài shēngwù yīxiào hé shípǐn lǐngyù jìnxíng le shíyàn, bìng pínggǔ le bùtóng láiyuán hé guīmó de MLLMs zài gèzhǒng tèdìng lǐngyù rènwù shàng de xìngnéng

8. Wèi zhīchí jìnfā yánjiū, wǒmen jiāng kāiyuán wǒmen de shíxiàn

1. In recent years, general-purpose multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have made rapid progress.

2. However, the application of these general models to specific domains, such as science and industry, remains relatively unexplored.

3. This paper systematically investigates the domain adaptation of MLLMs through post-training, focusing on data synthesis, training pipelines, and task evaluation.

4. We developed a visual instruction synthesizer capable of generating diverse visual instruction tasks from domain-specific image-caption pairs.

5. Our synthesized tasks outperform those generated by manual rules, GPT-4, and GPT-4V in enhancing the domain-specific performance of MLLMs.

6. We also employed a single-stage training pipeline to enhance the diversity of domain-specific tasks.

7. We conducted experiments in the biomedical and food domains and evaluated the performance of MLLMs from different sources and scales on various domain-specific tasks.

8. To support further research, we will open-source our implementation.
