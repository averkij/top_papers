1. This article discusses the challenges faced by large language models (LLMs) in handling long contexts.

2. Existing methods rely on data annotated by human experts or advanced models, which limits further development.

3. The authors propose a new method, called \ours, that enables LLMs to self-improve in long context reasoning.

4. This method involves sampling multiple outputs, using minimum Bayes risk scoring, and then performing supervised fine-tuning or preference optimization.

5. Experiments demonstrate that \ours is effective across multiple leading LLMs and outperforms methods that rely on data from human experts or advanced models.
