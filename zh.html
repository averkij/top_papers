1. This article discusses the potential long-chain reasoning capabilities of Large Reasoning Models (LRMs).

2. Previous research has shown that result-oriented reinforcement learning (RL) can occasionally trigger advanced reasoning behaviors, such as self-correction and verification phenomena, referred to as the model's "eureka moments.

3. " However, the timing and consistency of these behaviors are difficult to predict, limiting the reasoning capabilities of LRMs.

4. To address these issues, the authors propose a three-stage method that aligns the model with deductive, inductive, and abductive meta-abilities, thereby enhancing reasoning performance.
