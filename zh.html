1. This article discusses how existing end-to-end speech large language models (LLMs) typically rely on large-scale annotated data for training, while data-efficient training has not been deeply explored.

2. The article focuses on two fundamental issues between speech and text: the representation space gap and the inconsistency in sequence length.

3. The authors propose Soundwave, which employs efficient training strategies and novel architecture to address these problems.

4. The results show that Soundwave outperforms the advanced Qwen2-Audio in speech translation and AIR-Bench speech tasks while using one-fiftieth of the training data.

5. Further analysis indicates that Soundwave retains its intelligence in conversations.

6. The project's code can be found at https://github.
