Nevertheless, existing algorithms struggle in the low-label regime that characterizes many real-world applications. Flexible models overfit and rigid ones fail to adapt to the task at hand. We show that pretraining an in-context learner with a Perceiver-style architecture on synthetic data yields a model that can solve new tasks from a handful of labeled bags.
