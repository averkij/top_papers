By decomposing proximity into semantic, acoustic, and mixed axes using external reference encoders alongside each model's own internal encoder, we show that benign fine-tuning elevates Jailbreak Success Rate (JSR) from single digits to as high as 87.12%. Crucially, the dominant vulnerability axis and the relative risk of audio versus text fine-tuning are both architecture-conditioned -- determined by how each model's encoder and projector transform audio into the LLM's input space.
