HumanEval-V: Evaluating Visual Understanding and Reasoning Abilities of Large Multimodal Models Through Coding Tasks

1. This article discusses the value of coding tasks in evaluating large language models (LLMs) because they require understanding high-level instructions, complex reasoning, and implementing functional programs.

2. Although large multimodal models (LMMs) have made progress in visual perception and understanding, there is a lack of rigorous benchmarks for evaluating these models, particularly in tasks that emphasize visual reasoning.

3. To fill this gap, the authors introduce HumanEval-V, a new lightweight benchmark specifically designed to evaluate the visual understanding and reasoning capabilities of LMMs.

4. HumanEval-V includes 108 carefully designed elementary Python coding tasks, each with visual elements and detailed test cases.

5. The research found that even the most advanced LMMs face significant challenges in these tasks.
