1. This article introduces a video restoration model called SeedVR2.

2. It employs a diffusion-based method and an adaptive window attention mechanism to handle high-resolution videos.

3. Compared to existing methods, SeedVR2 provides high visual quality at a lower computational cost.

4. Through feature matching loss, it achieves stable and efficient adversarial training in a single step.

5. Experimental results demonstrate that SeedVR2's performance is on par with or surpasses existing video restoration methods.
