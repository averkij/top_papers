1. The emergence of large language models (LLMs) has driven a transformation in artificial intelligence, creating intelligent agents capable of complex reasoning, powerful perception, and diverse actions.

2. These agents are becoming increasingly important in AI research and practical applications, but their design, evaluation, and improvement face complex challenges.

3. This article reviews the modular and brain-like architecture of intelligent agents, combining principles from cognitive science, neuroscience, and computational research.

4. The article is divided into four parts: first, it explores the modular foundations of intelligent agents; second, it discusses mechanisms for self-enhancement and adaptive evolution; then, it investigates the collaboration and evolution of multi-agent systems; and finally, it emphasizes the importance of building safe and trustworthy AI systems.
