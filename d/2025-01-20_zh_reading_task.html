1. This article discusses an evolutionary search strategy for calculating the inference time of large language models.

2. The method, known as "Mental Evolution," employs language models to generate, recombine, and optimize candidate responses.

3. This approach eliminates the need to formally define underlying inference problems when a solution evaluator is available.

4. Under the constraint of controlling inference costs, Mental Evolution significantly outperforms other inference strategies, such as Best-of-N and Sequential Revision, in natural language planning tasks.

5. In the TravelPlanner and Natural Plan benchmarks, Mental Evolution solved over 98% of problem instances using Gemini 1.

6. 5 Pro without the need for a formal solver.
