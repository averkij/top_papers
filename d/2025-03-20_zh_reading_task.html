1. This article discusses the remarkable capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in complex tasks.

2. Recently, large reasoning models (LRMs) such as OpenAI o1 and DeepSeek-R1 have further enhanced performance in areas like mathematics and programming through techniques such as supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL).

3. However, longer reasoning sequences, while improving performance, also incur significant computational costs, a phenomenon known as "overthinking.

4. " The article provides the first systematic survey of advances in achieving efficient reasoning in LLMs and categorizes existing work into three directions: model optimization, reasoning output optimization, and input prompt optimization.

5. Additionally, the article explores the use of efficient data for training reasoning models, the reasoning capabilities of small language models, and evaluation methods and benchmark tests.
