1. Recent advancements in image relighting models have made consistent lighting effects possible.

2. However, video relighting still lags behind, primarily due to the high training costs and the lack of diverse, high-quality video relighting datasets.

3. Applying image relighting models frame-by-frame can result in inconsistent light sources and appearances, causing flickering in the generated videos.

4. We propose Light-A-Video, a training-free video relighting method.

5. It enhances lighting consistency through a consistent lighting attention module and a progressive light blending strategy, ensuring temporal coherence in the video.
