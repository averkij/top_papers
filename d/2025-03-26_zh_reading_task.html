1. This article introduces a new vision-language-action model framework called Dita.

2. It employs a Transformer architecture to directly denoise continuous action sequences through a unified multimodal diffusion process.

3. Dita achieves fine-grained alignment between denoised actions and original visual tokens by conditioning within the context.

4. This design significantly enhances the model's adaptability and robustness across different tasks and environments.

5. Experimental results demonstrate that Dita performs excellently in both simulated and real-world settings, particularly in complex, long-duration tasks.
