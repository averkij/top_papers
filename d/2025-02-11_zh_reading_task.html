1. This article discusses the problems with existing methods for detoxifying multilingual text, primarily the lack of parallel multilingual datasets.

2. The authors propose a pipeline method for generating parallel multilingual detoxification data.

3. They also introduce SynthDetoxM, a multilingual parallel text detoxification dataset containing 16,000 high-quality detoxified sentence pairs, covering German, French, Spanish, and Russian.

4. The data is sourced from various toxicity evaluation datasets and is rewritten using nine modern open-source LLMs with few-shot learning.

5. Experiments show that models trained on the synthetic dataset perform better than models trained on the human-annotated MultiParaDetox dataset when data is limited.

6. The authors have made their dataset and code publicly available to promote research on multilingual text detoxification.
