Enhancing the Reasoning Ability of Multimodal Large Language Models via Mixed Preference Optimization

1. 这篇文章讨论了现有开源多模态大语言模型（MLLMs）的训练过程和存在的问题。

2. 这些模型通常经过预训练和监督微调，但在分布偏移方面表现不佳，影响其多模态推理能力，特别是在Chain-of-Thought（CoT）性能上。

3. 为了解决这个问题，作者提出了一种偏好优化（PO）过程，并创建了一个高质量的多模态推理偏好数据集MMPR。

4. 此外，他们还开发了一种混合偏好优化（MPO）方法，提升了多模态CoT性能。

5. 实验结果显示，他们的模型在多个基准测试中表现更好，特别是在多模态推理任务中。

1. This article discusses the training process and issues of existing open-source multimodal large language models (MLLMs).

2. These models typically undergo pre-training and supervised fine-tuning, but they perform poorly in distribution shifts, affecting their multimodal reasoning capabilities, particularly in Chain-of-Thought (CoT) performance.

3. To address this issue, the authors propose a preference optimization (PO) process and create a high-quality multimodal reasoning preference dataset called MMPR.

4. Additionally, they develop a hybrid preference optimization (MPO) method, which enhances multimodal CoT performance.

5. Experimental results show that their model performs better on multiple benchmark tests, especially in multimodal reasoning tasks.
