Explanatory Instructions: Towards Unified Vision Tasks Understanding and Zero-shot Generalization

1. 这篇文章讨论了计算机视觉（CV）尚未完全实现自然语言处理（NLP）中观察到的零样本任务泛化。

2. 作者认为，CV 使用离散和术语化的任务定义（如“图像分割”），这可能是零样本任务泛化的主要障碍。

3. 为验证这一点，作者引入了解释性指令，通过详细的语言转换来定义 CV 任务目标。

4. 他们创建了一个包含 1200 万个“图像输入到解释性指令到输出”三元组的大规模数据集，并训练了一个自回归视觉语言模型（AR-based VLM）。

5. 该模型通过学习遵循这些指令，实现了零样本任务泛化。

1. This article discusses the fact that computer vision (CV) has not fully achieved zero-shot task generalization as observed in natural language processing (NLP).

2. The authors argue that CV's use of discrete and terminological task definitions (such as "image segmentation") may be a major obstacle to zero-shot task generalization.

3. To validate this, the authors introduce explanatory instructions, defining CV task objectives through detailed language translations.

4. They created a large-scale dataset containing 12 million "image input to explanatory instruction to output" triplets and trained an autoregressive vision-language model (AR-based VLM).

5. The model achieved zero-shot task generalization by learning to follow these instructions.
