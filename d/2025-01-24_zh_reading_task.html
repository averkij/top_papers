1. Multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) has made significant progress in addressing cooperative and competitive problems in various environments.

2. One of the main challenges of MARL is the need to explicitly predict the behavior of agents to achieve cooperation.

3. To tackle this issue, we propose the Shared Recurrent Memory Transformer (SRMT), which aggregates and globally broadcasts individual working memories, allowing agents to implicitly exchange information and coordinate actions.

4. We evaluated SRMT on a toy bottleneck navigation task and the POGEMA benchmark task set.

5. The results indicate that SRMT performs exceptionally well under sparse rewards and effectively generalizes to longer corridors not seen during training.
