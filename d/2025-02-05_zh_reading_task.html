1. This article discusses the application of Diffusion Bridge Models (DBMs) in image-to-image translation.

2. While DBMs hold promise, they suffer from slow inference speeds.

3. The authors propose a new distillation technique that can accelerate the inference process of DBMs.

4. This method can handle both conditional and unconditional DBMs and requires only the use of corrupted images for training.

5. Experimental results show that this technique can increase the inference speed of DBMs by 4 to 100 times and, in some cases, provide better generation quality than the original model.
